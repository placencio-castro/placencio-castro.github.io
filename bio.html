Bio

Dr. Matias Placencio-Castro is a Chilean educator, researcher, and data scientist with over a decade of experience in applied statistics, educational research, global health, and social policy. He currently serves as a Senior Social Sciences Research Data Analyst at the Research Program on Children and Adversity (RPCA) at Boston College. In this role, he leads the development and implementation of advanced statistical models, supports grant writing and research design, and mentors early-career researchers in quantitative methods.

His work is grounded in a commitment to scientific rigor, reproducibility, and the use of data to inform policy and practice in low-resource and post-conflict settings, with a focus on improving the health and well-being of vulnerable populations. Dr. Placencio-Castro earned his Ph.D. in Measurement, Evaluation, Statistics, and Assessment from the Lynch School of Education and Human Development at Boston College. He holds a Master’s degree in Sociology, a Bachelor’s degree in History, and a High-School Teaching Degree from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile.

His academic background showcases a unique combination of social science and education theory, global health, and quantitative methodologies, with intensive training in experimental designs, program evaluation, and advanced statistical modeling applied to clinical effectiveness trials and hybrid research designs that simultaneously test the impact of psychosocial interventions and the processes by which they are delivered.
